We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to differentiate you from other users and to remember your preferences while using the Website. This enables us to offer you a more seamless and personalised experience, while also helping us improve the quality and functionality of our services. On their own, cookies do not disclose your name, email address, or any personally identifiable information unless you voluntarily choose to provide such details, such as during registration. Cookies are designed to store limited data specific to each user. However, once you supply personally identifiable data through the Website, it may be associated with the cookie data for better personalisation.

We use cookies to understand how the Website is used and to refine its content and functionality. For instance, cookies may help personalise your experience (e.g., displaying your name when you return), save your login credentials in password-protected areas, and enable shopping cart functionality. In addition, cookies may be used to suggest relevant products, services, or programs. Some cookies may be placed by third parties, and we do not exercise control over how such third-party cookies are used.

Most cookies used on the Website are session cookies, which are deleted automatically once your browsing session ends. You have the option to decline cookies via your browser settings. However, disabling cookies may affect the usability of certain features and may require you to re-enter your login details more frequently. You may choose to keep cookies enabled for an improved user experience.
